General transfer function
A DC motor converts voltage to rotational speed. The general relationship between input voltage V(s) and output angular velocity Ω(s) is:

The coupling constant
K
Motor constant
0.50
The motor constant links the electrical and mechanical domains. It sets both the torque produced per amp of current (T = K·i) and the back-EMF voltage generated per unit of rotational speed (V_emf = K·ω). A larger K means a stronger motor that produces more torque per amp — but it also generates more back-EMF, which acts like a brake and limits top speed at a given voltage. This single number is the bridge between the circuit and the shaft.
